Chili Recipe

Description

  • Cooking Time: about 2 hours
  • Servings: 6
Probably the best chili you can taste (and cook). Recipe by Chef John.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 pounds lean ground beef
  • 2 teaspoons salt, plus more to taste
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 3 tablespoon ancho chile powder, or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (12-oz) bottle of beer
  • 1 cup tomato puree or crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cups water, or as needed
  • 2/3 cup diced green pepper
  • 2 cans pinto beans, drained and rinsed well

Directions

Step 1

  1. Place a pot over high heat
  2. Drizzle in vegetable oil
  3. Add diced onion, ground beef, and salt
  4. Break up meat with wooden spoon into small pieces as it browns
  5. After meat browns and releases its juices, continue stirring until the released liquid evaporates, about 4 minutes
  6. Reduce heat to medium-high
  7. Add garlic, chile powder, cumin, paprika, black pepper, and cinnamon
  8. Cook and stir until mixture begins to darken, 3 or 4 minutes

Step 2

  1. Stir in beer
  2. Add cocoa powder, oregano, cayenne, water, and tomato puree. Stir well.
  3. Bring to a simmer
  4. Adjust heat to medium-low. Simmer 30 minutes.

Step 3

  1. Stir in diced green pepper and pinto beans
  2. If mixture becomes too thick, add a bit more water
  3. Simmer until peppers are tender and flavors have blended, about 30 more minutes
And its ready to serve!

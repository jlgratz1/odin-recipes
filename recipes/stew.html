Beef & Guiness Stew

Description

  • Cooking Time:about 2,5 hours
  • Servings: 4
Something to cook for a St Patrick's Day. Or any other day.

Ingredients

  • 4 strips bacon, sliced into small pieces
  • 2,5 pounds beef chuck, cut in 2-inch pieces (tossed with black pepper and at least 1 teaspoon salt)
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can Guinness beer, or other dark beer
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 4 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • 3 carrots, cut in 1-inch pieces
  • 2 ribs celery, cut in 1-inch pieces
  • 2,5 cups chicken stock, or as needed
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 4 cups mashed potatoes, optional

Directions

Step 1

  1. Cook and stir bacon in a heavy skillet over medium-high heat until bacon is browned and crisp, 3 to 4 minutes.
  2. Turn off heat and transfer bacon into a large stew pot, reserving bacon fat in the skillet.

Step 2

  1. Season beef chuck cubes generously with 1 teaspoon salt and black pepper to taste.
  2. Turn heat to high under skillet and sear beef piece in the hot fat on both sides until browned, 5 minutes.
  3. Place beef in stew pot with bacon, leaving fat in skillet.
  4. Turn heat down to medium.
  5. Cook and stir onions in the retained fat in the skillet until lightly browned, 5 to 8 minutes.
  6. Season with a large pinch of salt.

Step 3

  1. Cook garlic with onions until soft, about 1 minute.
  2. Pour beer into skillet and stir with a wooden spoon, scraping up and dissolving any browned bits of food into the liquid.
  3. Pour cooking liquid from skillet into the stew pot.
  4. Stir in tomato paste, thyme sprigs, carrots, celery, sugar, 1/2 teaspoon black pepper, and enough chicken broth to cover.

Step 4

  1. Bring stew to a gentle simmer, stirring to combine.
  2. Reduce heat to low and cover pot.
  3. Simmer stew until beef is fork-tender, about 2 hours.
  4. Stir stew occasionally.

Step 5

  1. Remove cover and raise heat to medium-high.
  2. Bring stew to a low boil and cook until stew has slightly thickened, 15 to 20 minutes.
  3. Remove and discard thyme sprigs and adjust salt and pepper to taste.

Step 6

  1. Arrange mashed potatoes in a ring in a serving bowl.
  2. Ladle stew into the center of the potatoes.
